Pulihora

Description

Pulihora, also known as pulisoru, pulinchoru, puliyogare, puliyodarai, ambad baath, kokum rice, or simply tamarind rice or Pavan Maruthi, is a very common and traditional rice preparation in the South Indian states of Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Telangana.

The heart of this dish lies in the rich tamarind paste, derived from soaking a small ball of tamarind in warm water and extracting its pulp. This tangy essence is what infuses the rice with its distinctive taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • Small ball of tamarind (approximately the size of a lime)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup raw peanuts
  • 1-2 dry red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • Few curry leaves
  • 1-2 tablespoons grated jaggery
  • Salt
  • Chopped cilantro (coriander) leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Cook rice and let it cool.
  2. Rinse and soak basmati rice.
  3. Prepare tamarind paste from tamarind pulp and water.
  4. In a pan, temper mustard seeds, chilies, and curry leaves.
  5. Add tamarind paste, turmeric, and salt to the pan.
  6. Let the mixture simmer till it thickens.
  7. Mix in cooked rice and stir well.
  8. Slow cook (dum) for 30-40 mins.
  9. Garnish with roasted peanuts.
  10. Pulihora is ready to serve. Enjoy!
